UNDERSTANDING ENERGY DEREGULATION

In 1996, the Electricity Generation Customer Choice and Competition Act, was enacted. Allowing many PA citizens to choose their
own energy company. This meant PA residents could shop around for prices they were happy with, and no longer settle for the prices
they were offered.

Three years later, the Natural Gas Choice and Competition Act was passed in 1999 and for the first time Pennsylvania residents
were able to choose which companies supplied their natural gas commodities. For the first time in U.S. history, PA residents
can now legally choose their electricity and natural gas company. However, decades later, only several states in the U.S. have
deregulated energy markets that allow their residents to choose their energy company. PA is among the first states to provide
it's residents with the opportunity to choose their own energy plan. Although many PA residents are taking advantage of the
benefits of deregulation, we are passionate in providing the millions of others who have not yet take advantage of their freedom to choose.
